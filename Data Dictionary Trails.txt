A convention first: use stable text IDs as slugs, not numbers — estelle, bose-city, fc-ch1. They're readable when hand-typing on a phone, they don't break if you reorder, and a typo is obvious. Never reuse or repurpose an ID once data points at it.

characters.csv — one row per character, stable facts only

char_id — unique slug, permanent. The thing everything else references.
display_name — name shown on the icon (the publicly-safe one).
faction — affiliation safe to show from the start (e.g. Bracer Guild). Faction changes/reveals go in codex, not here.
first_met_location — loc_id where the party first meets them (your tracking highlight).
first_appearance_beat — beat_id of that first meeting.
icon — image filename for the map marker.

Note: I deliberately left status and true_identity out of this table. Both are spoilers and both change — they belong in codex with reveal gating, not as flat columns.

locations.csv — every place a marker can sit

loc_id — unique slug, permanent.
name — display name.
parent_loc_id — the containing place (bose-city → bose → liberl); blank for top level. Lets "in Liberl" and "in Bose Market" both resolve.
type — nation / region / city / district. Controls zoom level and how you place it.
map_x, map_y — coordinates on your map image (pixel or percentage; pick one and stay consistent).

beats.csv — the spine; the canonical timeline

beat_id — unique slug.
sequence — integer, strictly increasing. This is the real timeline. Sort and gate by this, never by date.
chapter — chapter label for grouping/display.
title — short human label ("Arrive in Bose").
default_location — loc_id where the party mostly is this beat; appearances inherit it.
approx_date — your interpolated in-world date. Display only. Revise freely; nothing depends on it.

appearances.csv — the heart; one row per character per continuous span with the party

char_id — who.
join_beat — beat_id they become active.
leave_beat — beat_id they leave. The greying happens on this beat; gone after it. A character who's with you the whole game just has the first and last beat.
location — loc_id, blank = inherit the beat's default, filled = override (this is your CS1 split case).
activity — what they're doing, short phrase.
source — game + scene the fact comes from, for verification.

One character can have several rows (leaves, rejoins, splits off). That's the point of intervals — don't fight it by forcing one row per character.

codex.csv — the progressive "what is known" entries, and your spoiler gate

char_id — who it's about.
revealed_at_beat — beat_id at/after which this entry is allowed to show. The single gating field for the whole site.
entry_type — identity / status / faction / bio / relationship. Lets you render "true identity revealed" differently from a bio note.
text — the entry itself.

Anything spoilery — true names, deaths, betrayals, faction flips — is a codex row with a revealed_at_beat, never a flat column elsewhere. That keeps one rule across the site: hide anything whose revealed_at is past the viewer's progress.
